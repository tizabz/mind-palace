# Mind Palace
#### Video Demo:  https://youtu.be/aFH7g58lSVo
#### Description:
Most of us run our minds the same way we did in school — react, remember what we can, decide on instinct. Mind Palace is a project built on a different idea: that thinking is a skill, and skills can be trained. It's a single home for three tools, each aimed at a different part of how we use our minds.

The first is Memory Palace — a game that teaches you classic memorization techniques the way a coach teaches a sport. Method of Loci, chunking, mnemonic linking — instead of reading about them, you'll practice them inside short, focused challenges that get harder as you improve. Memory Palace is still in development, but the core idea is simple: turn memory training into something you actually want to come back to.

The second is Higher Order Thinking — and this one is the longest road. It's the part of the project meant to teach techniques for thinking itself: how to reason, reframe, question assumptions, weigh evidence, separate signal from noise. There's a lot of ground here — critical thinking, mental models, structured problem-solving — and we're still mapping out how to teach it in a way that actually sticks. So this part isn't built yet. But it's where Mind Palace is ultimately heading.

The third, and the part that's ready today, is the Decision Making Matrix — a tool that helps you make real decisions with simple math instead of pure gut feeling. Let me walk you through it.

You open the app, sign in, and land on a grid of every decision you're working through. Each one is a card — title, short description, quick actions to open, edit, or delete. Create a new one and you're guided through three steps.

Factors. What actually matters about this decision? Salary, location, growth, culture — whatever fits. You weight each one, and the weights add up to a full picture of your priorities.

Ratings. You add your options — job offers, apartments, anything you're comparing — and rate each one against every factor on a color-coded one-to-ten slider.

Results. Mind Palace does the math — weight times rating, summed up — and ranks your options on a scoreboard. You see the winner, the runners-up, and a clear breakdown of why: which factor gave the top option its edge, which factor mattered most, where it pulled ahead of the rest.

Everything saves automatically, so you can come back later as new information arrives and refine your thinking.

Three tools. One mission. Mind Palace — train how you remember, how you think, and how you decide.
